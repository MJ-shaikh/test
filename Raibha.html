Raibha Toll Plaza

Agra Bypass Corridor

Raibha Toll Plaza is situated on an important four-lane National Highway corridor originating from NH-19 (old NH-2) and terminating at NH-44 (old NH-3) with a total project length of 32.80 kilometers.

The asset starts from the junction of NH-2 (Km 176+800) in the State of Uttar Pradesh at Km 0+000 and ends at the junction with NH-3 (Km 13+030) at Km 32+800 in the State of Uttar Pradesh.

The corridor includes one toll plaza namely Raibha Toll Plaza located at Km 10+800. The project acts as an important bypass to the city of Agra and serves as a major connectivity link between Delhi, Gwalior, Maharashtra, and the southern states of India.

The highway further supports east-west traffic movement by connecting traffic originating from western regions such as Gujarat and Jaipur towards eastern cities including Kanpur, Lucknow, and beyond. The road also functions as a connector between major National Highways in Uttar Pradesh and branches towards NH-52 (old NH-3) and NH-44 (old NH-7) at Gwalior.

The project supports advanced highway operations, electronic toll collection systems, ATMS, ITS infrastructure, CCTV surveillance, and efficient traffic management across the corridor.
